Malaysia Seat Projector

Malaysia parliament seat projector — GE15 baseline

Unity-collapse scenario: BN and PN vote totals are merged into one bloc in every seat, then BERSATU is carved back out as IPR (Ikatan Prihatin Rakyat) — 66% of the merged total in the 32 seats it actually won in GE15, 27% in the remaining seats PN contested. IPR stands wherever PN did nationally (its full GE15 footprint). Both carve fractions are the tuned values from the Negeri Sembilan model and are easy to retune if you have a better estimate. Baselines shown on each input are derived from this split, not the real GE15 result — there is no historical "BN+PN merged" or standalone national BERSATU result to check them against.

Methodology: Universal national swing (UNS) from GE15 results. BERSAMA has no GE15 baseline and is added as a flat UNS in every seat, like any other party. Taper scalar applied below 25% of each party's GE15 share — tapered votes redistribute to other named parties, not Others. Others fixed at seat-level GE15 residual. Muar (P146) attributed to Bloc Progresif (formerly MUDA); PH baseline 80 seats. Bloc Progresif uses MUDA's GE15 seat-level vote shares where contested; UNS from 0 elsewhere. PRM's GE15 votes (16 seats, all deposits lost) are included in the Bloc Progresif national baseline. PH's national baseline is derived by summing this file's own seat-level `ph` column (PKR+DAP+AMANAH only), not the official 37.95% headline figure — that figure also includes MUDA and UPKO, which this model books separately under Bloc Progresif and GRS respectively. Cross-checked against official GE15 results: GPS (4.26%), GRS (1.25%) and WARISAN (1.93%) all match the official totals exactly; this file's own seat-level gps/grs/war columns sum to slightly different figures (+0.84pp, +0.36pp, -0.29pp respectively), which points to seat-level data entry gaps rather than a bad constant, and hasn't been corrected here.
