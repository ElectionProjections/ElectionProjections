About the thresholds

Solo parties need 3% nationally to win proportional seats; coalitions need 10% combined. Inside a winning coalition, a member polling 1–3% gets no seats of its own — its votes pass proportionally to coalition partners above 3%. Below 1%, votes are simply lost.

Method. The Rosatellum elects 400 deputies three ways: 147 in single-member constituencies by plurality (FPTP), 245 in proportional seats calculated nationally, and 8 from constituencies abroad. Proportional seats use the official two-stage process — Hare quota with largest remainders, first among coalitions/parties that clear the threshold, then within each coalition among its qualifying lists. FPTP seats now use real per-constituency data: actual list votes for all 146 domestic single-member races (source: Italian Ministry of the Interior via onData, CC-BY 4.0; Valle d'Aosta's seat is handled separately) and uniform national swing applied seat-by-seat, exactly as for the Polish projector. The 2022 baseline produces CDX 124 / CSX 10 / M5S 9 FPTP winners, close to the certified 121/12/10. Seats within each coalition are split using the actual 2022 candidacy allocation. South Tyrol's People's Party (3 seats), South calls North (1), Aosta Valley (1), MAIE overseas (1), and the 7 overseas seats won by PD, FdI, and M5S are fixed at 2022 levels. National Future has no 2022 district data, so its FPTP support is estimated as a share of the local FdI+Lega vote proportional to its national polling share — a rough approximation for a brand-new party.

Sources. 2022 results: Italian Ministry of the Interior (Eligendo), processed by onData (CC-BY 4.0).
